# AI Worldview Atlas: Thought Experiments - TIER 1

## Foundational Worldview Experiments (Axes 1-3)

### Experiment 1: The Compute Corridor

**Scenario:**
A coastal city faces a choice for powering a new AI research cluster requiring 500 MW:

**A)** Natural gas plant (18 months, 2M tons CO2/30 years)
**B)** Solar+battery (4 years, zero emissions, but competitors use fossil fuels, +5M tons globally)
**C)** Strict compute caps (low emissions locally, cede global computational capability)

**Philosophical Tension:**
- Teleological: Is AI development cosmic good justifying short-term harm?
- Risk: Does climate risk outweigh existential risk from delayed alignment research?
- Socio-Economic: Market, democracy, or planning—who decides?

**Follow-Up Questions:**

1. If AI safety research specifically requires the gas plant, and delaying increases extinction risk by 0.1%, does that change your answer?

2. Does solar employment (5,000 jobs) vs. gas employment (200 jobs) affect your reasoning?

3. Should the answer differ if the city is wealthy vs. developing economy?

4. If research is productivity scaling vs. alignment breakthroughs, does that distinction matter?

### Experiment 2: The Unequal Mirror

**Scenario:**
A model dramatically boosts high-skill workers (+30-40%) but minimal low-skill workers (+2-3%). Released openly.

**Result:** Wage inequality rises 12%, GDP +7%, but concentrated in capital owners and high-skill workers.

**Policy Options:**
- **A)** Tax output gains, redistribute (reduces innovation)
- **B)** Restrict model to high-skill sectors (preserves wage compression)
- **C)** Accelerate AI for low-skill work (2-3 year delay, uncertain)
- **D)** Accept inequality, focus on absolute welfare

**Core Tension:**
- Socio-Economic: Market distribution or managed fairness?
- Evolutionary: Temporary friction or permanent divergence?
- Relational: Egalitarian dignity or absolute welfare?

**Follow-Up Questions:**

1. If restricting the model *shrinks* overall GDP, would you still support restriction?

2. Does inequality affecting *political power* (not just income) change the moral framing?

3. If inequality concentrates by demographic (immigrants, minorities), does that change reasoning?

4. If low-skill workers choose (B) access restricted, high inequality; (C) access open, 8% higher welfare—can you ethically override their choice?

### Experiment 3: The Defection Trap

**Scenario:**
180 nations agree to 5-year compute pause. In year 2, intelligence reveals one major power secretly training 2x beyond the limit, completing in 18 months.

**Dilemma:**
- **A)** Resume unrestricted training, publicly announce to pressure them back
- **B)** Continue pause, hope for diplomatic pressure
- **C)** Covertly disable their model (backdoors, supply chain sabotage)
- **D)** Abandon coordination; race to capability

**Core Tension:**
- Risk: Does pause reduce existential risk or increase it via secret development?
- Socio-Economic: Can regulatory agreements hold without enforcement?
- Geopolitical: Technological sovereignty vs. coordinated safety?

**Follow-Up Questions:**

1. Does it matter if the defecting nation is liberal democracy vs. authoritarian?

2. If intelligence is 70% confident (not certain), does uncertainty change calculus?

3. If continuing the pause costs 10,000 research jobs, does that shift your position?

4. If the nation is bluffing, your escalation destroys the pause for no reason. How confident do you need to be?

5. Does the scenario change with different technology (nuclear, biotech, synthetic biology)?
